## 0. Read this first — the honest version

Your instinct (high-volume content → WhatsApp → explainer video → call → close) is **the right shape**. But two things will make or break the 50-client goal, and your current plan is missing both:

1. **Organic alone cannot hit 50 in 10 weeks.** You have 682 followers. Even with great reels, organic reach is slow to compound and won't deliver the *volume* of qualified buyers you need before 30 Aug. **You must run paid ads** (Meta + a little YouTube) to your best content, targeting Pakistani expats in the GCC. Organic builds *trust and proof*; paid builds *volume and speed*. You need both.

2. **You must qualify leads BEFORE the sales call**, or you'll waste your closing time on people who can't pay $10k. Add a 3-question filter (budget / eligibility / timeline) between WhatsApp and the booked call.

Everything below is built around those two corrections plus your original funnel.

### The funnel math (this is the whole campaign in one table)

| Stage | Conversion | Needed to hit 50 |
|---|---|---|
| **Closed deposits** | — | **50** |

If your real close rate is higher (warm, referred, parent-backed leads often close at 30–40%), you need fewer calls — track and recalibrate weekly.

> **Biggest lever:** close rate on the call. Going from 20% → 30% cuts the leads you need by a third. Invest in the **sales process and proof**, not just more reels.

## 6. Sales: offer, calls, webinar, deposit

### The offer (make it a clear package, not "come study abroad")
Frame it as: **"Globally-recognised Dual-Degree MBBS+MPH, $10k/year, done-for-you admission + visa + housing, classes 15 September — and we guide you through all 5 steps."** Bundle your service so the deposit buys *certainty*, not just a seat.

### Deposit mechanics (you didn't specify — recommendation)
- Take a **seat-reservation / processing deposit** (e.g. a fixed registration fee) to lock the September seat and start documents. Make the **terms crystal clear in writing** (what it covers, refund policy). Clarity here directly lifts close rate — people pay when they're not scared of the money disappearing.
- Then the admission steps: documents → acceptance letter → tuition → visa & tickets.

### 1:1 call structure (15–25 min)
1. Rapport + "what made you reach out?" 2. Their goal & situation. 3. Confirm eligibility + funding. 4. Present the fit (use their answers). 5. Handle objections (scam/PMDC/safety/timeline). 6. **Ask for the deposit + give a reason to act now** (deadline, visa time, seats).

### Weekly webinar structure (45–60 min) — your scaling weapon
Story/credibility → the opportunity (why Azerbaijan, why now) → the program & proof (recognition, testimonials) → the 5-step process → cost breakdown → Q&A → **clear CTA + deadline + how to pay the deposit tonight.** Have your team DM every attendee right after.

### Top objections → pre-loaded answers
- **"Is it a scam?"** → "Verify it yourself: here's the WDOMS/PMDC listing + talk to current students." (Proof, not promises.)
- **"Will PMDC recognise it?"** → Show the *current* recognition status accurately (see compliance note below).
- **"Is it safe?"** → Muslim-majority country, halal food, mosques, current-student footage.
- **"Too expensive / can we pay in stages?"** → Compare to Pakistani private college total cost; explain the payment timeline.
- **"Let me think / ask my family."** → Invite the parents onto the next call/webinar directly.

## 8. Risks & compliance (read this — it protects the whole campaign)

- **Accreditation claims must be exact.** PMDC/HEC recognition of *specific* foreign medical universities changes over time and is the #1 thing serious Pakistani families (and regulators) check. **Verify the university's current PMDC/HEC recognised status before you advertise it**, and only claim what you can prove. Overclaiming → refunds, disputes, reputation damage, and students who can't sit the Pakistani licensing exam after 6 years. Show official listings, don't just assert them.
- **Deposit terms in writing** — refund policy, what it covers. Reduces disputes and *increases* conversions (people pay when it's clear).
- **Ad policy:** Meta restricts some education/financial claims; avoid "guaranteed" language. Keep testimonials truthful and permissioned.
- **Data:** you're collecting names/numbers — handle the lead list responsibly.
- **Capacity check:** can your team actually *service* 50 admissions (documents, visas, housing) by 15 Sep? Closing 50 you can't deliver is worse than closing 35 well. Make sure operations scale with sales.
